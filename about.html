MOPARRU ORPHANAGE

Originally founded in 2011 Moparru orphanage is situated in the southeast of India, in the district of Andhra Pradesh. Caring for between 30-40 children daily, the orphanage's primary aim is to ensure children are safe, happy, and receive a good education. Providing 24/7 care for the children are the Pallikonda family, headed up by Raju and his wife Lavanya.

Each day, they make sure that the children have adequate access to water, food, clothing, and go to school. They both love what they do, and their two daughters Amulya and Anusha also care for the children when they are not studying themselves.

Typical Daily Schedule

6am - Wake up and get ready for school, preparing any materials and completing any of their own chores

8am - Breakfast

8:45am - School

4pm - Back from school and playtime

6pm - Homework

8pm - Dinner

9pm - Evening church

9:30pm - Bedtime

The children range from 7-18 years old, some of whom have been at the orphanage for most of their life. Please visit our profile page to read some of the stories of our children.

We hope you will join us in helping to care for these children in need. Many of them come from very poor families, some do not have any family at all.
